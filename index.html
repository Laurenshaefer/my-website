MEET THE GIANTS

Late Cretaceous
T. Rex
Tyrannosauridae · Theropod

The undisputed apex predator of the Late Cretaceous. With bone-crushing bite force and keen binocular vision, T. rex remains the most iconic dinosaur ever discovered.

Length 12–13m
Weight 9 tonnes
Diet Carnivore

Sound by Paul (PWLPL) via Pixabay

Late Jurassic
Brachiosaurus
Brachiosauridae · Sauropod

A colossal gentle giant that browsed treetops with its extraordinary long neck. One of the tallest animals to ever live, weighing as much as 12 African elephants.

Length 26m
Weight 60 tonnes
Diet Herbivore

Sound via Pixabay

Late Cretaceous
Triceratops
Ceratopsidae · Ceratopsian

Armed with three imposing horns and a massive bony frill, Triceratops was a formidable herbivore that shared its world — and often crossed horns — with T. rex itself.

Length 9m
Weight 12 tonnes
Diet Herbivore
