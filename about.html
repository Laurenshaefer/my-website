Hi, I am Lauren

Dinosaur Enthusiast & Hobby Researcher

I have been interested in dinosaurs for as long as I can remember. Growing up, I was captivated not just by how big or fearsome they were, but by the extraordinary diversity hidden across millions of years of prehistory — the differences in how they moved, what they ate, where they lived, and how their bones tell the story of entire ecosystems long gone. This site is my way of sharing that passion with anyone curious enough to look.

My focus is on the different types of dinosaurs and what makes each group truly unique. I have loved studying their skeletons and exploring everything that sets them apart — from the hollow bones of theropods to the armored frills of ceratopsians. Whether you are a lifelong dino nerd or just starting to explore, you are in the right place.

What I Know

AREAS OF FOCUS

Species Identification

Distinguishing dinosaur species by their anatomical features, proportions, and defining skeletal characteristics.

Skeleton Recognition

Reading fossil bone structures to identify species, posture, locomotion patterns, and evolutionary relationships.

Geographical Relation

Connecting dinosaur species to the ancient continents, habitats, and climate zones where their fossils are found.

Recognizing Groups

Classifying dinosaurs into their correct clades — theropods, sauropods, ornithischians, and beyond — based on shared traits.
